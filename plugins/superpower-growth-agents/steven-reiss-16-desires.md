# Steven Reiss 16 Fundamental Desires

Reference guide for mapping consumer motivations to advertising copy. Based on Steven Reiss's research on intrinsic motivations.

## The 16 Desires Framework

### 1. Power
**Core Drive**: Desire for influence, leadership, and control over others
**Health Marketing Angle**: Control over your body, health decisions, and outcomes
**Example Copy**:
- "Take back control of your health"
- "Stop letting your body run the show"
- "You decide what happens next—not your genetics"

### 2. Independence
**Core Drive**: Desire for self-reliance and freedom from dependence on others
**Health Marketing Angle**: Not needing doctors for every question, self-sufficiency in health monitoring
**Example Copy**:
- "Know your body without waiting for a doctor's appointment"
- "Your health data, on your terms"
- "Stop depending on annual checkups to tell you something's wrong"

### 3. Curiosity
**Core Drive**: Desire for knowledge and truth
**Health Marketing Angle**: Understanding what's really happening inside your body
**Example Copy**:
- "What's really causing your afternoon crashes?"
- "The answers hiding in your bloodwork"
- "Finally understand why you feel the way you do"

### 4. Acceptance
**Core Drive**: Desire for approval and belonging
**Health Marketing Angle**: Validation that you're on the right track, being part of a health-conscious community
**Example Copy**:
- "Join 50,000+ members who've optimized their health"
- "Your doctor said you're fine. Your biomarkers tell a different story."
- "The test that validates what you've always suspected"

### 5. Order
**Core Drive**: Desire for organization, stability, and predictability
**Health Marketing Angle**: Clear roadmap for health, structured protocols, organized health data
**Example Copy**:
- "A clear plan. Not guesswork."
- "100+ biomarkers organized into one actionable report"
- "Finally, a system for your health—not random supplements"

### 6. Saving
**Core Drive**: Desire to collect and accumulate
**Health Marketing Angle**: Building health data over time, tracking progress, accumulating knowledge
**Example Copy**:
- "Track your progress over months. Watch your numbers improve."
- "Build a complete picture of your health, one test at a time"
- "$199/year vs. $100k concierge medicine—same insights, fraction of the cost"

### 7. Honor
**Core Drive**: Desire to be loyal and follow a moral code
**Health Marketing Angle**: Doing right by your body, responsibility to family, integrity in health choices
**Example Copy**:
- "Your family deserves you at your healthiest"
- "The responsible choice for your health"
- "Stop ignoring what your body's trying to tell you"

### 8. Idealism
**Core Drive**: Desire for social justice and fairness
**Health Marketing Angle**: Healthcare access equality, democratizing preventive health
**Example Copy**:
- "The test that billionaires get. Now for $199."
- "Preventive health shouldn't be a luxury"
- "What Silicon Valley executives know about testing—now available to everyone"

### 9. Social Contact
**Core Drive**: Desire for companionship and social interaction
**Health Marketing Angle**: Connection with health advisors, community of health optimizers
**Example Copy**:
- "24/7 access to our clinical team—whenever you need them"
- "Questions? Text our team anytime."
- "You're not doing this alone"

### 10. Family
**Core Drive**: Desire to raise and care for children/loved ones
**Health Marketing Angle**: Being healthy for family, detecting hereditary risks, longevity for loved ones
**Example Copy**:
- "Be there for the moments that matter"
- "Catch it early. Be there for your kids."
- "Your health affects everyone who loves you"

### 11. Status
**Core Drive**: Desire for social standing and prestige
**Health Marketing Angle**: Being ahead of the curve, premium health optimization, what successful people do
**Example Copy**:
- "What top performers do differently for their health"
- "The biohacker's secret: comprehensive blood work"
- "Elite health testing. Not elite prices."

### 12. Vengeance
**Core Drive**: Desire to get even, to compete and win
**Health Marketing Angle**: Proving doubters wrong, beating genetic odds, outperforming expectations
**Example Copy**:
- "Your doctor said you're fine. Prove them wrong."
- "Don't let your genetics write your story"
- "Fight back against aging with data"

### 13. Romance
**Core Drive**: Desire for sex, beauty, and romantic relationships
**Health Marketing Angle**: Energy for relationships, hormonal optimization, vitality
**Example Copy**:
- "Optimize the hormones that drive your energy (and everything else)"
- "Feel like yourself again in the bedroom"
- "The vitality you had in your 20s—backed by data"

### 14. Eating
**Core Drive**: Desire for food and appetite satisfaction
**Health Marketing Angle**: Understanding food sensitivities, metabolism, nutrition optimization
**Example Copy**:
- "Why that 'healthy' diet isn't working for you"
- "Your metabolism, decoded"
- "Stop guessing what to eat. Know what your body needs."

### 15. Physical Activity
**Core Drive**: Desire for physical exercise and movement
**Health Marketing Angle**: Athletic performance, recovery optimization, training intelligence
**Example Copy**:
- "Train smarter with your biomarkers as your guide"
- "Recovery data that actually helps"
- "Stop overtraining. Start optimizing."

### 16. Tranquility
**Core Drive**: Desire for emotional calm and freedom from anxiety
**Health Marketing Angle**: Peace of mind, reducing health anxiety, certainty about your body
**Example Copy**:
- "Stop worrying. Start knowing."
- "The peace of mind that comes from actually knowing your numbers"
- "Replace health anxiety with health certainty"

## Using This Framework

### For Ad Copy
1. **Identify 1-2 primary desires** that match your target persona
2. **Lead with desire-based language** in headlines and hooks
3. **Connect features to desires**, not just outcomes
4. **Test combinations** - some desires pair well (Curiosity + Tranquility, Power + Independence)

### For Creative Briefs
1. **Research first**: Find which desires your persona actually expresses on Reddit/forums
2. **Don't assume**: Different segments have different primary desires
3. **Be specific**: Generic desire language doesn't convert. Use persona's exact words.

### Desire Combinations for Health Testing

**High-Performing Parents**: Family + Tranquility + Order
**Biohackers/Optimizers**: Curiosity + Power + Status
**Anxious Health Seekers**: Tranquility + Curiosity + Independence
**Athletes/Fitness Enthusiasts**: Physical Activity + Power + Status
**Busy Executives**: Independence + Order + Saving (time)
**Proactive Preventers**: Honor + Family + Idealism
