# Visual Direction Guidelines

## ‚úÖ APPROVED Visual Styles

- Real photography (lifestyle, clinical, portraits)
- UGC-style content (authentic, unpolished)
- Clean data visualizations (charts, graphs)
- Split-screen comparisons (before/after states)
- Text overlays on photography
- Product/dashboard screenshots

## ‚ùå PROHIBITED Visual Styles

**NEVER recommend:**
- Cartoons or cartoon-style illustrations
- Animated characters or mascots
- Comic-style graphics
- Stylized/illustrated people
- Infographic-style illustrated scenes

**Why:** Superpower is a preventive health company requiring credibility and professionalism. Cartoon/illustrated styles undermine medical authority and don't resonate with our 30+ educated female audience who expect data-driven, serious health solutions.

**When writing visual direction:**
- Focus on real people, real moments, authentic emotion
- Specify photography style (e.g., "Natural light portrait of woman in her 30s looking directly at camera with determined expression")
- Use "clean, minimal" not "illustrated" or "graphic"
- Reference real campaign aesthetics (Hims, Modern Fertility, Parsley Health)
